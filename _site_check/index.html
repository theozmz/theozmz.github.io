    Welcome to my homepage!

    Hi!👋👋👋 I am a Master student in the School of Computing and Data Science at the University of Hong Kong. Before that, I received my Bachelor’s degree in the School of Computer Science and Technology at Tongji University. My research interest lies in machine learning, LLM, and agentic AI.

    I am actively seeking research assistant, research internship and such opportunities to deepen my expertise in large language models and their applications in real-world scenarios. With a strong foundation in machine learning and a growing passion for diffusion models, I am eager to contribute to cutting-edge research projects while developing my technical and analytical skills. My long-term goal is to pursue a PhD in Computer Science.

    Although I am currently a Taught Postgraduate (TPG) student with limited opportunities to engage in research and minimal publications to date, I have never ceased seeking collaborative projects and am deeply committed to continuous learning. You can find out more details in my 👉CV👈. I did some readings and made some toy projects after class, you can find some notes in 👉Blogs👈. Although I have few publications, I still have some research experiences, you can find them in 👉Publications👈.

    The most profound technologies are those that disappear. They weave themselves into the fabric of everyday life until they are indistinguishable from it. – Mark Weiser (The Father of Ubiquitous Computing)

    My research sensibility is shaped by Mark Weiser’s quiet observation that the most profound technologies are those that disappear. My work is guided by a simple conviction: that the most enduring forms of computation are those we cease to notice. I study machine learning, harness engineering, and diffusion models not only for what they produce, but also for the unknown functions they allow us to uncover, that is, the latent order within high-dimensional noise. What draws me is the possibility of reducing entropy not by imposing rigid control, but by learning the quiet regularities already present in the world and gently amplifying them. I want to build systems that lower the cognitive and environmental noise around us, not add to it. A successful technology, in this view, is one that makes the space it inhabits calmer, simpler, and less demanding of our attention than it was before.

    I have been working at the perspectives of machine learning, harness engineering, and diffusion models, and I am more interested in what they can quietly hold.🤔 I think of machine learning not as a spectacle of prediction but as a material for building environments that do not demand attention.😵 Harness engineering, for me, names the discipline of restraint with the careful shaping of computational behavior so that it aligns with human rhythms rather than interrupting them.🦄 And I am drawn to diffusion models precisely because their generative power can be turned away from the task of making images, text and even code toward something more ambient: the slow, diffuse shaping of conditions rather than the production of artifacts.🎼 Rather than what else can we make, it is better to ask what can we finally let go of? I want to build systems that recede, that become indistinguishable from the background of a room or a day, so that people might simply live more easily inside them.

    Research Experiences

    2026

    • Unifying Temporal and Structural Credit Assignment in LLM-Based Multi-Agent Prompt Optimization [openreview]: This is a short-time voluntary RA experience with Prof. Wenhao Li at Tongji University. The work lasts from Jan. 2026 to Mar. 2026. In this paper, the temporal and structual credit assignment problems in reinforcement learning are adopted to LLM-based multi-agent systems prompt optimization. Text gradient method is applied during a debate process. The idea was provided by the professor. I wrote most parts of the paper, drew some figures and tables, and ran all the experiments. The paper is under review by UAI.

    • A Survey of Automatic Prompt Engineering: An Optimization Perspective [arxiv]: This is a short-time voluntary RA experience with Prof. Wenhao Li at Tongji University. The work lasts from Sep. 2025 to Jan. 2026. In the paper, LLM context engineering is categorized into three optimization perspectives: prompt optimization, structural optimization, and memory optimization. I drew some figures, collected papers, summarized methodologies, finally wrote structual and memory optimization parts. The paper was rejected by IJCAI due to citation issues.

    2025

    • BIRD-CRITIC NL2SQL Dataset Alignment Annotation [website]: This is a short-time voluntary RA experience with Prof. Reynold C.K. Cheng at HKU. The work lasts from Sep. 2025 to Oct. 2025. I checked over 1500 instances systematically, identified and classified 6 common categories of data quality issues, then rewrote 300+ problematic SQL queries.

    • An LLM Agent for Reasoning and Decision-Making Based on PRMs: This is my undergraduate thesis. The work lasts from Nov. 2024 to May. 2025. The research purpose is inspired by ChatGPT-o1, which is popular at that time. The goal is to explore the reasoning ability improvement method behind ChatGPT-o1. Several post-training methods, such as SFT, and several reinforcement learning methods, such as PPO and GRPO, are applied.
